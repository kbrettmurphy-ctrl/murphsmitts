What Is a Full Glove Relace?

A full baseball glove relace involves completely removing all existing lace from the glove and replacing it with new, high-quality leather lace. This includes the web, fingers, palm, heel, and wrist areas — not just a single broken section.

Over time, glove lace dries out, stretches, and weakens. Even if it has not snapped yet, brittle lace can fail during normal play. A full relace restores structure, pocket integrity, and proper tension throughout the glove so it performs the way it was designed to.

Rather than patching individual areas as they break, a complete relace ensures consistent strength and longevity across the entire glove. It is often the most cost-effective way to extend the life of a quality glove for multiple additional seasons.

Why Quality Lace Matters

All relaces are performed using premium leather lace from Tennessee Tannery. Cheap lace stretches, cracks, and fails early. Quality lace holds tension, maintains pocket shape, and performs under game conditions.

Who Needs Baseball Glove Relacing?

A glove likely needs relacing if the lace appears dry, cracked, stretched, or discolored. Loose fingers, a collapsing pocket, or shifting web are also signs that the lace is no longer holding proper tension.

Players who practice multiple times per week, play travel ball, or compete in high school and adult leagues put significant stress on glove lace. Even well-maintained gloves eventually require relacing simply due to normal wear.

If you are unsure whether your glove needs a full relace or minor repair, I will evaluate it honestly and recommend the most practical option.
